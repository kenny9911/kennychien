AI Application Launch

One use case, taken seriously — from board-level thesis to software running in production.

Most enterprise AI initiatives die in pilot purgatory: a promising demo, an enthusiastic steering committee, and no path to production. The cause is rarely the model. It is unowned risk — unanswered questions about data boundaries, evaluation, security review, and who carries the pager when the agent is wrong.

A Launch engagement is designed as the opposite of a pilot. We pick one use case with measurable economics, define what "working" means before writing a line of code, and drive it through the full gauntlet — legal, security, evaluation, rollout — so your organization learns the entire path, not just the demo.

“The goal is not a demo. The goal is the organizational muscle to ship the next ten applications without me.”

Operating principles

01

Economics before architecture

We model the value of the use case first — minutes saved, revenue unlocked, risk retired — and let that number discipline every technical decision.

02

Evaluation is the product

Before the first prompt, we build the eval set: golden tasks, failure taxonomies, acceptance thresholds. A system you cannot score is a system you cannot ship.

03

Smallest deployable loop

We ship a thin, end-to-end slice to real users in weeks, then widen it — never a six-month build toward a big-bang reveal.

04

Your team writes the code

My job is leverage, not dependence. Your engineers pair on every component and own the system from day one of production.

Engagement structure

WEEKS 1–2

Frame

Use-case selection, value model, data and risk audit, definition of done. Output: a launch memo your CFO and CISO both sign.

WEEKS 3–6

Build

Evals first, then the application: retrieval, tools, agent loops, human-in-the-loop checkpoints. Weekly demos against the eval set.

WEEKS 7–10

Harden

Red-teaming, security review, observability, cost envelopes, rollback paths. The unglamorous work that separates demos from systems.

WEEKS 11–12

Launch & transfer

Production rollout to a real cohort, runbooks, and a trained team. I leave; the system stays.

Frequently asked questions

How long does an AI Application Launch take?

Six to twelve weeks depending on scope, structured in four phases: frame, build, harden, and launch and transfer. A thin end-to-end slice reaches real users within weeks — never a six-month build toward a big-bang reveal.

Who from our side needs to be involved?

A leadership sponsor who owns the value model, plus a small delivery team who will own the system. Your engineers pair on every component and write the code; my job is leverage, not dependence. If building capability across a whole team is the deeper goal, forward deployed engineering may be the better fit.
