Field notes.

The operational record of the agentic era — what shipped, what broke, and what the token bill actually bought.

Field Notes is my blog on enterprise AI in production. It collects deployment write-ups, launch post-mortems, and working notes on agentic ontology and token economics — the things I learn while shipping, written down before the sting fades. Everything here comes from real engagements, not thought experiments.

The longer thesis essays live in Ideas, and recorded conversations live on the podcast. This page is the shorter, more practical companion to the consulting work itself. Posts appear in English or Chinese, whichever language the argument was born in; each one is labelled so you know before you click.

Everything here was learned in production — on real data, behind real permissions, with real users watching.

Token 是成本单位，业务结果才是价值单位

Tokens are the cost unit. Business outcomes are the value unit.

The entire AI infrastructure chain — models, inference, chips, data centers — is repricing around tokens. Application companies must not worship them. This essay argues that the real dividend of the AI era is converting the right tokens, with the right model, context, and ontology, into measurable, reusable business outcomes — and gives enterprise buyers three questions for judging any AI application. Common questions

What does the Field Notes blog cover?

Field Notes is Kenny Chien's blog on enterprise AI in production. It covers deployment write-ups, agentic application launches, agentic ontology in practice, token economics, and the working notes and scripts behind real engagements.

How is the blog different from the Ideas section?

Ideas holds the longer thesis essays — the arguments. Field Notes is the shorter, operational record: what shipped in a specific deployment, what broke, and what the fix was. Read Ideas for the why; read Field Notes for the how.

What languages are posts written in?

Posts appear in English or Chinese, whichever language the argument was born in. Every post is labelled with its language on this page, so you know before you click. The latest essay, on token economics, is in Simplified Chinese.

How do I get new posts?

There is no mailing-list machinery yet. Email kenny.chien@gmail.com with the subject "Subscribe to Field Notes" and you will get new posts by reply as they are published.

Replies are the best part of publishing. Send me the paragraph you disagree with — or the deployment story you think I should write up next.
